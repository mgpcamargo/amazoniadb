# Sources to verify

Real institutions relevant to AmazoniaDB's domains, not yet in `data/catalog.js`. These are leads, not verified entries — each one still needs someone to confirm the exact URL, read the actual access/reuse terms (don't guess), and write a plain-language description, per `CONTRIBUTING.md`.

Pick an unchecked item, verify it, submit it through the issue form or a pull request like any other entry. Check the box once it's merged into the catalog.

Current coverage is Brazil-heavy (10 of 12 entries) with one global and one pan-Amazon source. Everything below is a start at the other 8 Amazon countries.

## Peru
- [ ] **Geobosques** — geobosques.minam.gob.pe — MINAM's national forest-loss monitoring platform: annual deforestation maps + early-warning alerts, stated as free public access. → *Land use & infrastructure*

## Colombia
- [ ] **IDEAM / SMByC** — ideam.gov.co — national Forest and Carbon Monitoring System; quarterly deforestation alerts, annual forest-loss figures since 2012. → *Land use & infrastructure*

## Bolivia
- [ ] **ABT** — abt.gob.bo — Autoridad de Fiscalización y Control Social de Bosques y Tierra, the official body for Bolivia's deforestation monitoring and forest-land oversight. → *Land use & infrastructure*
- [ ] **GeoFAN / VISTA** (via Fundación Amigos de la Naturaleza) — name confirmed through Mongabay reporting (June 2026) on deforestation tracking across Bolivia's 48 Amazonian indigenous territories; exact URL not yet found. → likely *Peoples, territories & culture*

## Ecuador
- [ ] **MAATE / Sistema Nacional de Monitoreo de Bosques** — snmb.ambiente.gob.ec — national forest monitoring system; deforestation and land-cover maps back to 1990. → *Land use & infrastructure*

## Also open
Every lead above is forest/deforestation monitoring, because that's the most publicly documented category across these countries — it was the easiest to find quickly. Biodiversity, hydrology, health/livelihoods, and governance sources for these same countries are completely unexplored. So is Brazil beyond the current 10.
